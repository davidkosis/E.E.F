LAUTECH Union Tells LAUTECH ASUU To Withdraw From ASUU Strike

In a recent development regarding the ongoing ASUU strike, the LAUTECH Union has made a bold request for the LAUTECH ASUU chapter to withdraw from the industrial action. The call came amidst the desire to address the specific issues and challenges faced by the Ladoke Akintola University of Technology (LAUTECH) and its students.

The ASUU strike, which has been ongoing for several weeks, has impacted academic activities across universities in Nigeria, including LAUTECH. The suspension of classes, examinations, and other academic engagements has raised concerns among students and stakeholders, leading to varying opinions and reactions.

Union's Stance and Rationale

The LAUTECH Union, representing the collective interests of students and non-academic staff, believes that a separate approach should be adopted to address the specific issues faced by LAUTECH. The university's unique circumstances, including its joint ownership by Oyo and Osun States, demand a targeted resolution that takes into account the institution's financial, administrative, and infrastructural challenges.

According to the Union's statement, withdrawing from the ASUU strike would provide LAUTECH the opportunity to negotiate and engage with relevant authorities on its specific needs. This, in turn, could lead to a faster resolution of the challenges and a quicker return to academic activities.

Impact on Students and the Academic Calendar

The prolonged ASUU strike has taken a toll on students' academic progress and mental well-being. The disruption of the academic calendar has resulted in uncertainty and anxiety among students, especially those in their final years who are eager to complete their studies and graduate.

Additionally, the delay in academic activities has also raised concerns about the quality and completion of the syllabus, which may affect the overall learning outcomes and assessment of students. The Union's call for LAUTECH ASUU to withdraw from the strike seeks to mitigate these negative impacts on students and restore the university's academic stability.

Way Forward

As the situation unfolds, it is crucial for all stakeholders, including university management, ASUU, the LAUTECH Union, and the governments of Oyo and Osun States, to engage in constructive dialogue and find a lasting solution to the challenges faced by LAUTECH. A collective effort is needed to prioritize the education and well-being of students and uphold the reputation of the university.

It remains to be seen how the ASUU and the respective authorities will respond to the LAUTECH Union's request. As the discussions continue, students and the academic community hope for a resolution that allows LAUTECH to resume its academic activities promptly.
